# Daily Cardio Routine for Fat Loss

**Created:** March 23, 2026  
**Goal:** Accelerate fat loss while preserving muscle mass  
**Current Stats:** 291.8 lbs, 42.9% body fat, eating 1,500 cal/day

---

## The Philosophy

At a **1,500 calorie intake** with a **~3,800 calorie burn** on workout days, the deficit is doing the heavy lifting. Cardio is supplemental for:
- Heart health (critical at 291 lbs)
- Metabolic adaptation prevention
- Mood and stress relief
- Recovery enhancement (blood flow)

**Key Rule:** Keep it low intensity. NO HIIT. Heart rate under 135 bpm.

---

## Workout Days (Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, Friday)

### Cardio Protocol
**Total Time:** 20-30 minutes

| Timing | Duration | Intensity | Purpose |
|--------|----------|-----------|---------|
| **Pre-Workout** | 10-15 min | Moderate | Warm up, get heart rate up, mental prep |
| **Post-Workout** | 10-15 min | Easy | Cool down, walk out the pump, bring HR down |

### Pre-Workout Options
- Treadmill: 3.0-3.5 mph, slight incline (2-5%)
- Stationary bike: Moderate resistance, steady pace
- **Goal:** Break a light sweat, elevate heart rate to 110-120 bpm

### Post-Workout Options
- Treadmill: 2.8-3.2 mph, flat or 2% incline
- Walking outside (if gym is near home)
- **Goal:** Active recovery, flush lactic acid

**Calories Burned:** ~250-350 total

## Rest Day Cardio Options

### Option 1: The Treadmill Grinder (Recommended)

```
Duration:     60 minutes
Speed:        3.0 mph (steady - DO NOT RUN)
Incline:      8-12%
Heart Rate:   120-130 bpm (conversational pace)
Calories:     ~650-750

Execution:
- Set treadmill to 10% incline, 3.0 mph
- Find a show, podcast, or audiobook
- Bring a towel (you WILL sweat)
- Sip water throughout
- If HR exceeds 135, drop incline to 8%
- If too easy at 30 min, bump to 12% incline

Why This Works:
- Incline walking = glute and calf activation
- Low impact (critical at current weight)
- Steady-state burns fat, not muscle
- Sustainable for 60 minutes
```

### Option 2: The Outdoor Walk

```
Duration:     60 minutes
Pace:         3.5-4.0 mph (brisk, not jogging)
Heart Rate:   115-125 bpm
Calories:     ~500-600

Execution:
- Find a route with hills if possible
- Morning fasted = more fat oxidation
- Or evening to de-stress from work
- WEAR GOOD SHOES (291 lbs = joint stress)
- Flat, supportive surface preferred

Why This Works:
- Fresh air and sunlight
- Less boring than treadmill
- Natural terrain challenges different muscles
- Can be combined with errands (walk to store)
```

### Option 3: The Split (Easier on Joints)

```
Morning:  30 min walk (fasted or after light snack)
Evening:  30 min walk (after dinner, helps digestion)
Total:    60 minutes
Calories: ~600-700 (same as continuous)

Execution:
- Morning: Easy pace, wake up the body
- Evening: Slightly faster, de-stress
- Same total calories, easier on feet/knees
- Good for those with joint pain

Why This Works:
- Easier mentally (two 30-min sessions vs one 60)
- Spikes metabolism twice
- Less foot fatigue
- More flexible scheduling
```

## Critical Rules & Warnings

### The "DO NOT" List

1. **DO NOT RUN**  
   At 291 lbs on 1,500 calories, running = knee/ankle destruction + muscle cannibalism. Keep both feet on the ground.

2. **DO NOT DO HIIT**  
   Your body is already stressed from the deficit. HIIT spikes cortisol = muscle loss. Steady-state only.

3. **DO NOT EXCEED 135 BPM**  
   If you can't hold a conversation, you're going too hard. Heart rate cap is 135 bpm.

4. **DO NOT SKIP REST DAYS**  
   4 heavy lifting days + daily cardio = overtraining risk. Rest days are when you burn fat AND recover.

5. **DO NOT FORGET WATER**  
   60 minutes at 291 lbs = 1-2 lbs water loss. Hydrate before, during, and after.

## Warning Signs to Watch For

If these appear, **reduce cardio immediately** (cut to 30 min or skip):

| Symptom | What It Means | Action |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| Strength dropping in gym | Can't hit planned weights | Cut cardio, eat more |
| Constant fatigue/brain fog | Overreaching | Add rest day, reduce volume |
| Sleep getting worse | Cortisol too high | Stop all cardio for 2-3 days |
| Joint pain (knees/ankles) | Too much impact | Switch to bike/swimming |
| Dizziness during cardio | Blood sugar crash | Eat before cardio |
| Obsessive hunger | Deficit too aggressive | Add 200-300 calories |

**The deficit is aggressive. Listen to your body.**

## Final Notes

**This routine is aggressive.** 

You're eating 1,500 calories, burning 3,200-3,600+ daily, and lifting heavy 4x/week. That's body recomposition on hard mode.

**The cardio is OPTIONAL for fat loss.** The deficit alone will drop weight. But the cardio:
- Protects your heart
- Keeps metabolism humming
- Prevents metabolic adaptation
- Builds the habit for maintenance phase

**If you're dying, cut back.**  
30 minutes is still good.  
45 minutes is still good.  
Any movement is better than no movement.

**But if you can handle 60 minutes...**  
The results will come fast.
